I have been involved in decorating since I bought my first house in 2001 at the age of 18. While my friends were planning study or travel options, I was going to house inspections in my school uniform and agonising over the perfect paint colour.

During the next decade, I studied at Hospitality school, obtained a Bachelor of Economics and a Graduate Diploma in Education, worked in Economic Development, travelled, lived abroad, and settled back in Hobart working as a Maths and Business Teacher.

It was on my way home from the airport that I purchased my second property and I spent the next 5 years restoring, renovating and styling my 1920s cottage.

But it wasn't until turning 30 and suffering from Chronic Fatigue Syndrome that I re-assessed my priorities and the penny finally dropped – I wanted to spend my time doing something I really loved – helping people, creating, and seeing projects come to fruition without a web of bureaucracy and red tape.

I bought a seaside apartment and rebuilt a much more peaceful, productive and prioritised lifestyle.

This is where I currently live and work.
